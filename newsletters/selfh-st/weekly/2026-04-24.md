# Self-Host Weekly (24 April 2026)

> Big tech giveth, and big tech taketh away

## Weekly Highlights

Microsoft's Xbox division [earned a ton of goodwill with the community this week](https://news.xbox.com/en-us/2026/04/23/we-are-xbox/?ref=selfh.st) after its new CEO brought a slew of changes to the platform, including a rebrand from 'Microsoft Gaming' back to 'Xbox', Game Pass price decreases, a [new logo](https://x.com/Xbox/status/2047435350524477484/photo/1?ref=selfh.st), and an [upcoming partnership with Discord](https://www.theverge.com/news/916787/microsoft-discord-xbox-game-pass-partnership?ref=selfh.st) *(ew, David)*.

What does this have to do with self-hosting? Nothing, but it's been a fantastic distraction from a few other shady things they're up to that will quietly impact many of us, like:

- [Automatically opting GitHub CLI users into telemetry collection](https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/22/github_opts_all_cli_users/?ref=selfh.st)
- [Trying to make 'vibe working' a thing](https://www.theverge.com/news/917328/microsoft-agent-mode-vibe-working-office-word-excel-powerpoint?ref=selfh.st) with a new Copilot mode in its Office suite
- [Large-scale voluntary staff reductions](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2026/apr/23/meta-microsoft-tech-ai-layoffs?ref=selfh.st) amid increased AI productivity

But before you pat yourself on the back with the smug sense of superiority so many of us feel as we look down on our SaaS-ridden counterparts, the grass wasn't necessarily greener on the other side this week:

- **Bitwarden CLI** was [compromised in an upstream supply chain attack](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/bitwarden-cli-compromised-in-ongoing.html?ref=selfh.st)
- The **KitchenOwl** (grocery lists, recipes) GitHub repo went down after the [developer's account was mistakenly flagged for malicious activity](https://mastodon.world/@tombursch/116353967312974565?ref=selfh.st) (although it [seems to be back up now](https://github.com/TomBursch/kitchenowl?ref=selfh.st))
- **Mastodon's** flagship server was [hit by a major DDoS attack](https://status.mastodon.social/cmo730jrm05ailojla22drifc?ref=selfh.st)
- Nextcloud, Euro-Office, LibreOffice, Collabora, and others are [still bickering](https://nextcloud.com/blog/euro-office-license-compliance-and-what-open-source-means/?ref=selfh.st) over what I've now dubbed **The Office Wars **(read my [4/3](https://selfh.st/weekly/2026-04-03/) and [4/10](https://selfh.st/weekly/2026-04-10/) intros for additional context)

But, hey – somebody developed a [customizable remote control card for Home Assistant](https://github.com/thecodingdad/grid-remote-card?ref=selfh.st) that I'm looking forward to configuring this weekend, so that's something.

Happy **selfh.st**/ing!

## Content Spotlight

Meet **Anchor**, an offline-first self-hosted note-taking platform. A relative newcomer in the space, **Anchor **places an emphasis on simplicity with a sticky note-like interface for organizing and managing thoughts and ideas. Features includes a rich text editor, color-based tags, sharing, attachments, backgrounds, pinning, archives, trash, and single sign-on via OIDC.

**Anchor** can be [easily deployed via Docker](https://github.com/ZhFahim/anchor?ref=selfh.st#option-1-using-pre-built-image-recommended) and doesn't require additional containers to get up-and-running.

## Command Line Corner

Append the **-p** flag (--parents) to the **mkdir** command to create entire directory trees at once rather than using multiple **mkdir** commands to create nested folders:

```bash
$ mkdir -p appdata/wiki/bookstack
```
