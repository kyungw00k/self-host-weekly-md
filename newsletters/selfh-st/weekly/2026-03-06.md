## Weekly Highlights

Occasionally, I find it helpful (especially during slow news weeks) to take a step back and observe what's happening across the broader tech landscape to gain a better appreciation for self-hosted projects and communities.

For example, Microsoft made headlines (multiple times) again this week after [banning the word *microslop* on their Discord server](https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/03/02/microsoft-gets-tired-of-microslop-bans-the-word-on-its-discord-then-locks-the-server-after-backlash/?ref=selfh.st) amid unsubstantiated claims that [Windows 12 will be AI-focused and subscription-based](https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/03/05/microsoft-isnt-launching-a-subscription-based-windows-12-ai-os-in-2026-the-rumors-are-just-ai-hallucinations/?ref=selfh.st). Just a few days later, rumors also began circulating that [OpenAI is developing an internal GitHub alternative](https://www.techrepublic.com/article/news-openai-internal-github-alternative/?ref=selfh.st) in response to the platform's numerous outages over the last several months.

And then there's the [viral McDonald's fiasco](https://www.forbes.com/sites/johnbbrandon/2026/03/04/mcdonalds-ceo-eats-burger-gets-roasted-by-burger-king-in-viral-post/?ref=selfh.st), which saw the fast food chain's CEO try his best to convince the world he actually consumes his company's food (spoiler: he doesn't) in an ad announcing the return of a popular sandwich. Can you imagine a world in which open source and self-hosted software developers weren't users of the tools they build?

In other news:

- The viral AI chat bot **OpenClaw** [surpassed React as the most-starred project on GitHub](https://www.star-history.com/blog/openclaw-surpasses-react-most-starred-software?ref=selfh.st) (see also: [OpenClaw Exposure Watchboard](https://openclaw.allegro.earth/?ref=selfh.st))
- **Palmr**, a file transfer app that launched and gained popularity last year, has been [officially been archived on GitHub](https://github.com/kyantech/Palmr/issues/418?ref=selfh.st)
- **Anthropic** launched a [Claude for Open Source Program](https://claude.com/contact-sales/claude-for-oss?ref=selfh.st) that will gift six free months of Claude Max to maintainers of popular projects (and [some have already made the cut](https://x.com/databasus/status/2028819112500031939?ref=selfh.st)!)
- *Frenck* of **Home Assistant** fame developed an integration that [allows users to play the original DOOM directly from their dashboards](https://github.com/frenck/home-assistant-doom?ref=selfh.st)
- A new-ish MQTT platform called [**BunkerM**](https://github.com/bunkeriot/BunkerM?ref=selfh.st) fell prey to the AI marketing hype with a rebranded AI domain – [that's currently parked](https://www.bunkerai.dev/?ref=selfh.st)?
- **Ente** (mentioned last week for their [Locker launch](https://ente.io/blog/locker/?ref=selfh.st)) was back at it again this week with the [announcement of Ensu](https://ente.io/blog/ensu?ref=selfh.st), an offline, privacy-focused LLM app
- [**Myrient**](https://myrient.erista.me/?ref=selfh.st), a video game preservation site with over 390 terabytes of retro game ROMs, announced they're shutting down due to insufficient funding and increasing hardware costs
- [This mad lad](http://ewaste.fka.wtf/?ref=selfh.st) spun up a static web site – on a disposable vape...

## Content Spotlight

Meet **Versity S3 Gateway**, a self-hosted bridge for accessing local filesystems through object storage APIs. A lightweight alternative to heavier platforms like [Garage](https://garagehq.deuxfleurs.fr/?ref=selfh.st) or [RustFS](https://rustfs.com/?ref=selfh.st), **Versity S3 Gateway** sits between local files and projects that require object storage, translating API calls on-the-fly. The platform currently supports a number of storage systems and includes a basic web interface for interacting with files and 'buckets'.

**Versity S3 Gateway** can be easily deployed via [Docker](https://github.com/versity/versitygw?ref=selfh.st#run-the-gateway-in-docker) or [single command from the command line](https://github.com/versity/versitygw/wiki/Linux-Install?ref=selfh.st).

## Command Line Corner

Preface a command with a single space to prevent it from being recorded in the terminal's history:

```bash
$ ls -l
$  cd ..
$ cd /
$ history
  1 ls -l
  2 cd /
```
