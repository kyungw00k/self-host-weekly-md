## Weekly Highlights

Every so often, I revisit the collection of apps I monitor for [selfh.st/apps](https://selfh.st/apps) to flag projects that are no longer maintained and prevent them from being recommended to readers (some day I'll get around to creating a separate view for them).

Given the relatively slow news week, I thought I'd pay tribute to a few of these projects.

In alphabetical order:

- [Commento](https://gitlab.com/commento/commento?ref=selfh.st): Popular commenting platform for blogs before built-in comments became the norm
- [CouchPotato](https://github.com/CouchPotato/CouchPotatoServer?ref=selfh.st): Media downloader before the *arrs were a thing
- [Gelli](https://github.com/dkanada/gelli?ref=selfh.st): A very early and capable Jellyfin music client for Android that pioneered the way for many modern clients
- [kbin](https://github.com/ernestwisniewski/kbin?ref=selfh.st): The OG content aggregation service that was popular during the Reddit exodus and the foundation for [mbin](https://github.com/mbinOrg/mbin?ref=selfh.st)
- [Librum](https://github.com/Librum-Reader/Librum?ref=selfh.st): Minimal, modern e-book reader known for its clean interface
- [Pinry](https://github.com/pinry/pinry?ref=selfh.st): The closest 1:1 alternative to Pinterest (image boards) before [bookmarking and read-later platforms](https://selfh.st/apps/?tag=Bookmarks%2CRead%20Later) took off
- [Self-Hosted Gateway](https://github.com/hintjen/selfhosted-gateway?ref=selfh.st): Tunneling alternative to Cloudflare and Tailscale (that now links to a [Minecraft server project](https://gethomerun.app/?ref=selfh.st)?)
- [Sheetable](https://github.com/SheetAble/SheetAble?ref=selfh.st): Music sheet organization platform that (to my knowledge) doesn't have a modern open source replacement
- [Yacht](https://github.com/yacht-sh/yacht?ref=selfh.st): Container management platform before container management platforms were cool (although I'm not sure this one ever left beta)

Similarly, here are a few projects that made a splash at launch but never received more than 1-2 months of development (what we'd call *the AI effect* today):

- [Gmail Cleaner](https://github.com/Gururagavendra/gmail-cleaner?ref=selfh.st): Web-based interface for cleaning Gmail mailboxes
- [MedAssist](https://github.com/njic/medassist?ref=selfh.st): Medication tracking and reminders
- [Metadata Remote](https://github.com/wow-signal-dev/metadata-remote?ref=selfh.st): Web-based music metadata editor
- [pad.ws](https://github.com/coderamp-labs/pad.ws?ref=selfh.st): Whiteboard as an IDE
- [Subvert](https://github.com/aschmelyun/subvert?ref=selfh.st): Video AI subtitle, chapter, and summary generator

And lastly, an honorable mention: the [DumbWare](https://www.dumbware.io/?ref=selfh.st) suite of apps. They aren't technically unmaintained, but much of the hype has fizzled after a short burst of app launches last year. According to the project's Discord, the developer recently had a baby and is taking time off to be present, so no shade from me.

In other news:

- The **Open Home Foundation** released [their first ever annual report](https://www.openhomefoundation.org/blog/the-first-of-many-our-2025-annual-report/?ref=selfh.st) (tl;dr – 2 million active Home Assistant installations, new partners, and assumed ownership over key projects like HACS, Music Assistant, and Z-Wave JS)
- The new **Euro-Office** project [dropped its first release](https://nextcloud.com/blog/press_releases/sovereign-office-suite-euro-office-to-release-june-9/?ref=selfh.st) and the **LibreOffice** team had [something to say about it](https://blog.documentfoundation.org/blog/2026/06/08/an-open-letter/?ref=selfh.st)
- **Pangolin** (remote access) added support for [web-based remote access](https://pangolin.net/news/1-19-release?ref=selfh.st) (SSH, RDP, and VNC) in its [v1.19.0 release](https://github.com/fosrl/pangolin/releases/tag/1.19.0?ref=selfh.st)
- The co-founder of **Plex** and primary developer of [Plexamp](https://www.plex.tv/plexamp/?ref=selfh.st) quietly launched a new headless music player called [Caldera Music](https://caldera.homes/music/?ref=selfh.st)
- Due to hardware shortages, the going price for a **Raspberry Pi 5** with 16GB of memory [is now $350](https://www.adafruit.com/product/6125?src=raspberrypi&ref=selfh.st)
- A new open source git forge called [gitdot](https://gitdot.io/?ref=selfh.st) recently launched and is [taking the moral high ground](https://gitdot.io/faq?ref=selfh.st) against GitHub – despite the [15+ CLAUDE.md files in the project's main repository](https://gitdot.io/bkdevs/gitdot/files?ref=selfh.st)
- A Lemmy user tipped me off to this [cheeky CLAUDE.md file](https://codeberg.org/irelephant/kittygram/src/branch/main/CLAUDE.md?ref=selfh.st) belonging to [**Kittygram**](https://codeberg.org/irelephant/kittygram?ref=selfh.st), a self-hosted front end for Instagram
- The latest version of **Infuse** (media streaming client for Apple devices) [added support](https://community.firecore.com/t/infuse-8-4-6-now-available/60018?ref=selfh.st) for secondary Jellyfin and Emby addresses
- I asked the developer of **Poznote** (note-taking) for a new dark theme and [he delivered in record time](https://github.com/timothepoznanski/poznote/releases/tag/6.18.0?ref=selfh.st) (and with a truly groan-worthy pun)

Happy **selfh.st**/ing!

## Content Spotlight

Meet **LeafWiki**, a Markdown-based, no-frills wiki platform for individuals and small teams. Built as a straightforward alternative to existing apps like [Outline](https://old.reddit.com/r/selfhosted/search?q=outline%20help&restrict_sr=on&include_over_18=on&sort=relevance&t=all&ref=selfh.st), **LeafWiki **boasts a simple deployment and file-based storage system that relies on a single SQLite database for metadata tracking. Other features include various access modes (internal, public read, open editing), tree navigation, manual page ordering, tags, backlinks, and concurrent editing.

**LeafWiki** can be [easily deployed via Docker](https://github.com/perber/leafwiki?ref=selfh.st#docker-compose) and doesn't require any additional containers for functionality.
